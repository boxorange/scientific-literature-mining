Welcome to our literature mining project.

Text mining is the process of extracting meaningful information from a large volume of unstructured text data. This project builds a pilot system for data collection and extraction from the scientific literature related to X-ray spectroscopy. NSLS-II and other facility users at BNL can quickly look up reference structures during their experiments at a beamline. This portal is divided into two parts: information retrieval through article search of our collection, and a classification of papers according to transition metals and XAS edges. Figures contained in the paper are extracted, together with caption; text relevant to each figure is also extracted from the paper and presented.


Literature Data Statistics

Articles are retrieved from publishers under their text mining policy.

This collection contains structured articles focused on X-ray spectroscopy from the following publishers - Elsevier, PubMed Central (PMC), Springer, Royal Society of Chemistry (RSC) - and unstructured articles - Institute of Physics (IOP). While these publishers are providing bulk download access at no additional cost through the BNL library licenses, the American Chemical Society (ACS) has chosen to charge additional fees for text mining purposes. As a result of these prohibitive fees, ACS articles are not included in this collection.
